    Healthy Summer Snacks

    5 minute read

    Summer food can be the tastiest - barbecues, ice cream, sno cones, fair food.

    But our tasty treats usually take hours to prepare and are not all that healthy

    That’s the conundrum with summer. We want to be healthy and have the energy to spend all day outside, and we also want to enjoy tasty stuff. Our sugar loving brains may be crying out for a trip to the ice cream parlor, but our wallets and future selves want us to make other choices. While we are no experts on food here, we have spent years with this very same problem. Over the years, we’ve been able to come up with some tricks for healthy summer snacks that keep us (and our families) going.

    Prepare ahead

    We often want to run out of the house for a day at the lake, beach, mountain, and it would never happen if we needed to stop along the way to pick up snacks. Mostly because we would faff the day away planning out when and where we would eat next. As time has passed, we’ve found some tried and true snacks that we just keep on hand ready to go out with whoever is going. Most of these are not seasonal (because fresh fruit and what not is all at risk of being smooshed). The gist is buying in bulk stuff that you will eat and then splitting it up into single servings immediately.

    Trail mix

    Granola, hippie food, whatever. A mix of nuts or nuts and fruit or nuts and chocolate can really make the day go by easier. Not only does it keep well, but it is light and does not require sitting down in a dry place. The varieties of mixes that you can put together are endless. Ours goes really fast when we make a special batch of honey or cinnamon nuts in the mix.

    Jerky or dried meats

    Dried foods last forever (or at least seem to) if you can avoid noshing them up. Dried meats especially can pack a protein filled punch for the adventurous. There are so many premade varieties in the store, but most of those are so expensive and so limited in flavor - admittedly, this seems to be getting better. You can make jerky in a warm oven with thinly sliced meats - marinate in your favorite flavors to shake things up. We like to go with combinations of spicy and peppery, and every once in awhile, sweet barbecue - surprisingly good on turkey.

    Salad it up

    Joking aside, summer is a great time for salad. The greens just keep growing, and you can get lettuces and cabbages for cheap (or for the effort of growing them). We so, so, so recommend keeping some meats leftover from barbecues to use in salad in the coming week. This works, we promise - even teenage boys* are happy to eat steak salad. (and aren’t they a hard to please bunch) The trick we use here is to not have the same salad every time. So, one day, we have egg salad with ripe grape tomatoes, and we won’t serve that one again for awhile - taco salad (with no silly shell) anyone? It’s as simple as trading out traditional dressing for salsa and ensuring you have limes and avocado on hand. Here are 13 salads for the 13 weeks of summer:

    • egg salad
    • steak salad
    • taco salad
    • cobb salad
    • strawberry salad
    • beet salad (check out how the New Zealanders do this)
    • coleslaw
    • teriyaki chicken salad
    • caesar salad
    • corn salad
    • melon salad
    • potato salad (you knew we would sneak that in)
    • barbecue chicken salad

    Cook in batches

    No one wants to heat up the house in the summer. That’s why we are out grilling and busy noshing on salad, right? But some things we do want to cook, and we might want to do it in the house. We try batch cooking items that keep pretty well, so that we can get by with the least amount of artificial house heating as possible. You may have different things that you use a lot, but we go through so many eggs, chicken, and potatoes (pretty much every kind) that these get special treatment.

    Eggs

    We boil eggs by the dozen, often two dozen, and keep them in the fridge for eating as is - what is simpler than a boiled egg? or turning into deviled eggs or egg salad in a hurry.

    Chicken

    We bake and saute chicken in several pound batches to have different flavors on hand for sandwiches and salads. It is so much easier to just do it all at once than to cook chicken a couple of times per week.

    Potatoes

    We boil and bake potatoes in bunches - as much as we can fit - and then save the rest in the fridge and freezer. Potatoes keep very well, but we never really have to test it because they are so easy to eat hot or cold.

    Get crazy with the berries

    Berries are sweet and, freshly picked, embody summer’s promises. Early in the season, strawberries lead the way - flushing markets with their bright red skin. Cherries and blueberries follow with their deeper rich flavors. Then we get boysenberries, raspberries, blackberries, and more to take us through fall. The great thing about these berries is that they are tastiest right away, but somehow we can only eat so many. Go ahead, try to gorge yourself on berries.

    We like to pick berries and sometimes do the all you can eat while you are at the farm kinda thing. We also like the much easier option of picking up a pint or two at the market and using them right away.

    Three delicious things to do with berries

    We recommend the first one the most because it is the least amount of work.

    1. Eat them as is, as snacks in themselves, with granola/cereal, or on top of salads or yogurt.
    2. Mix them into stuff. Not even kidding. Have you ever spread raspberry butter on a piece of toast - it’s amazing and just requires smooshing some raspberries into some softened butter. Also, the obvious - smoothies and milkshakes are better with berries. A few recipes: Strawberry Banana 1 pint strawberries, 1 banana (preferably frozen), 2 cups milk (any kind) Cherry Coconut 2 pints cherries, 1 can coconut milk (with cream) Cloud Berry 3 pints berries (any kind), 1 cup iced cream, 1 cup whipping cream (or coconut cream)
    3. Can them. Yes, you knew we were going to suggest canning. You can make jam or jellies, or can them with framboise for an ice cream topper like no other. Who says you have to save your canned goods. Enjoy them now. Now!

    Here’s wishing you a delicious summer.
